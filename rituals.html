Rituals

Seasonal observances & community gatherings

Rituals are under development. They will be tied to natural cycles and designed for real life—not idealized life.

The Seasons

Spring — Renewal & Growth

March through May. The world wakes up. First camping trips. Wildflowers in the foothills. Birds return. This is the season of emergence and beginning again.

Rituals to be developed drawing from: Passover themes of renewal, Daoist spring symbolism, indigenous spring ceremonies, Nowruz, equinox traditions.

Summer — Abundance & Pilgrimage

June through August. The high Sierra opens. Backpacking season. Long days, abundant light, community gathering. This is the season of immersion and fullness.

Rituals to be developed drawing from: Shavuot (harvest), indigenous summer gatherings, Buddhist summer retreats (vassa), solstice traditions worldwide.

Autumn — Gratitude & Letting Go

September through November. Return to urban rhythm. Days shorten. Leaves turn where they can. This is the season of gratitude, reflection, and release.

Rituals to be developed drawing from: Sukkot (harvest, impermanence), Yom Kippur (reflection), indigenous fall ceremonies, Buddhist impermanence teachings, equinox traditions.

Winter — Rest & Deep Time

December through February. Dormancy. Snow in the mountains. Ski trips to Mammoth. Long nights for reading and inner work. This is the season of rest, ancestors, and the deep dark.

Rituals to be developed drawing from: winter solstice traditions (Yule, Dongzhi, Inti Raymi), Hanukkah (light in darkness), Buddhist winter retreats.

Community Gatherings

The Weekly Gathering

Inspired by Shabbat but freed from theistic framing. A weekly or bi-weekly evening—Friday is traditional, but any evening works—dedicated to presence with people you love.

The elements: candles or natural light. Food prepared with intention. No phones, no screens. Conversation that matters. An optional reading from the canon. A moment of silence or gratitude before eating.

This is not performance. This is showing up.

Specific blessings, invocations, and conversation prompts to be developed, drawing from Jewish Shabbat structure, Islamic iftar, Buddhist sangha meals, Sikh langar, indigenous feast traditions.

Feast Days

Three to four significant gatherings per year, tied to solstices, equinoxes, or other meaningful dates. Larger than weekly gatherings—potluck meals, outdoor elements when possible, readings and invocations, time for both celebration and reflection.

Specific feast day formats to be developed.

Pilgrimage

The annual return to the Sierra Nevada—or whatever wild place calls to you—is not a vacation. It is pilgrimage. Treat it accordingly.

Pilgrimage rituals and frameworks to be developed.
