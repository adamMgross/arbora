The Eight Principles

2³ — resonant across traditions

Each principle will be expanded with source traditions, key quotes, daily practice examples, and optional invocations. This is the draft framework.

1. Honor the Web of Life

All beings are interconnected. Your actions ripple outward through systems you cannot see.

Sources to develop: Buddhist dependent origination, Daoist interconnection, indigenous reciprocity ethics, Simard's mycorrhizal networks, systems thinking (Meadows).

2. Dwell in Deep Time

You are a temporary expression of ancient processes. Think in scales of mountains and forests, not news cycles.

Sources to develop: scientific cosmology, indigenous oral histories, Buddhist impermanence (anicca), Stoic cosmic perspective (Marcus Aurelius), McPhee's geological writing.

3. Seek Clarity Through Practice

Meditation, movement, silence, and study sharpen your perception and reduce your suffering.

Sources to develop: Buddhist mindfulness (vipassana), Daoist zuowang, Stoic prosoche (attention), contemplative Christian tradition, Sam Harris' secular meditation.

4. Give and Receive in Reciprocity

Nothing is owed; everything is a gift. Take only what you need. Give back more than you take.

Sources to develop: Kimmerer's Honorable Harvest, Buddhist dana (giving), Confucian reciprocity, Stoic justice, indigenous gift economies, Andean ayni.

5. Remember Your Ancestors

You carry forward the survival, wisdom, and stories of those who came before. Honor them by living fully.

Sources to develop: Jewish yizkor and remembrance traditions, Confucian filial piety, Shinto ancestral rites, indigenous ancestor veneration, Day of the Dead, Ashkenazi diaspora consciousness.

6. Gather and Break Bread

Community, hospitality, and presence with others are sacred. Show up for your people.

Sources to develop: Jewish Shabbat, Islamic iftar, Buddhist sangha, Sikh langar, indigenous feast and council circles, Junger's Tribe.

7. Question Everything; Revere the Search

Study, debate, and intellectual honesty are spiritual acts. Never accept simplified narratives.

Sources to develop: Jewish Talmudic tradition, Socratic method, Buddhist Kalama Sutta, Stoic logic, Aristotelian inquiry, Postman's media ecology.

8. Stand Humbly Before the Unknowable

You are small. Mystery is not a problem to solve but a reality to inhabit. Wonder is enough.

Sources to develop: Daoist mystery, apophatic theology (Christian and Sufi), Buddhist sunyata (emptiness), Jewish Ein Sof, Stoic amor fati, Epicurean ataraxia.
