Daily Practices

Loose invocations, not rigid prescriptions

Practices are suggestive frameworks, not rules. Adapt them to your life. The goal is to bring the qualities of presence, interconnection, and reverence into ordinary hours.

Morning

The Porch Ritual

Sit with your tea, your people, your animals. Face the sun. Leave your phone inside. Let your mind be where your body is. This is already sacred—you are simply acknowledging it.

Optional invocations to be developed: rotating themes of gratitude for ancestors, awareness of nearby trees and plants, presence with loved ones, deep time awareness, seasonal awareness. Drawn from adapted Jewish brachot, Buddhist morning chants, Daoist greetings, indigenous dawn prayers.

Morning Walk

Ten to twenty minutes through your neighborhood. Notice specific trees, listen for birds, observe seasonal changes. Walk without earbuds. Let the world speak first.

Movement

Silent Movement

One or two workouts per week in silence—no podcasts, no music with words. Just your body, your breath, the sound of your feet or the weight in your hands. This is where meditation lives for people who move.

Optional invocations to be developed, drawing from Daoist internal arts, Buddhist walking meditation, Stoic embodied practice, Boyd Varty's tracking philosophy.

Contemplation

Sitting

Five to ten minutes. Non-dogmatic. You can follow your breath, observe your thoughts, or simply sit in silence. The form matters less than the consistency.

Guidance to be developed drawing from vipassana, Daoist zuowang (sitting and forgetting), Christian centering prayer, Sufi muraqaba.

Study

Read from the canon. Listen to an audiobook on your commute. Engage with ideas that expand your sense of what is real and what matters. Study is spiritual practice.

Nature Observation

Learn the trees in your neighborhood. Notice when the birds change. Watch the light shift through the seasons. You don't need to be in the Sierra to practice attention to the living world.

Evening

Reflection

Five minutes before sleep. What did you notice today? Which of the eight principles did you embody? Which did you neglect? No judgment—just observation.

Presence

Put your phone in another room. Be with the people and animals who share your home. This is not a spiritual exercise. This is the point.
