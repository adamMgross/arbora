We seek wisdom from all human traditions that illuminate our relationship to the living world, to our ancestors, and to the vast unfolding of time.

Arbora is a syncretic, non-theistic religion built on the humble curation of humanity's deepest wisdom. It draws from Buddhism, Daoism, Stoicism, Judaism, indigenous traditions from every continent, classical philosophy, Sufism, Christian mysticism, and the empirical sciences, weaving their most resonant threads into a coherent practice for those who find the sacred in trees, in silence, in community, and in the long arc of geological time.

The canon is never closed. Any tradition that speaks to interconnection, presence, reciprocity, and wonder is welcome here.

The Root

Eight values from which everything grows

I

Interconnection

All things are related. Humans are embedded in ecological, temporal, and ancestral systems — woven into them completely.

II

Deep Time

Awe for geological processes, evolution, forests, mountains, the slow work of millions of years.

III

Ancestral Consciousness

Honoring those who came before. Carrying forward their stories, their survival, their wisdom.

IV

Presence & Clarity

Silence, meditation, embodied awareness. Freedom from constant input and distraction.

V

Reciprocity

Mutual obligation, freely chosen. What you take, you give back.

VI

Intellectual Rigor

Study, debate, curiosity. Never accepting simplified narratives. Seeking truth even when uncomfortable.

VII

Community & Gathering

Breaking bread together, rituals with others, human connection as sacred. Show up for your people.

VIII

Wonder

Recognition that we are small. Mystery is a reality to inhabit, and inhabiting it is enough.

The Eight Principles

Draft — to be expanded with source texts, invocations, and practice guidance

  1. Honor the Web of Life All beings are interconnected. Your actions ripple outward through systems you cannot see.
  2. Dwell in Deep Time You are a temporary expression of ancient processes. Think in scales of mountains and forests.
  3. Seek Clarity Through Practice Meditation, movement, silence, and study sharpen your perception and reduce your suffering.
  4. Give and Receive in Reciprocity Nothing is owed; everything is a gift. Take only what you need. Give back more than you take.
  5. Remember Your Ancestors You carry forward the survival, wisdom, and stories of those who came before. Honor them by living fully.
  6. Gather and Break Bread Community, hospitality, and presence with others are sacred. Show up for your people.
  7. Question Everything; Revere the Search Study, debate, and intellectual honesty are spiritual acts. Never accept simplified narratives.
  8. Stand Humbly Before the Unknowable You are small. Mystery is a reality to inhabit, and inhabiting it is enough. Wonder is enough.
